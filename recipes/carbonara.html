Linguine alla Carbonara

Home

There’s something truly magical about a perfect plate of Carbonara — the way the creamy sauce wraps around each strand of pasta, the aroma of crispy guanciale, and the sharp bite of Pecorino Romano. This dish captures the essence of Roman cuisine: simple ingredients, prepared with precision and love.

Ingredients (for 2 servings)

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) guanciale (or pancetta, if unavailable)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g (1 cup) Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients.

    Start by bringing a pot of salted water to a boil. While you wait, cut the guanciale into thin strips and grate the Pecorino Romano until fine and fluffy.

  2. Cook the guanciale.

    In a large pan, cook the guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy and golden, releasing its flavorful fat. Turn off the heat and let it rest while the pasta cooks.

  3. Cook the pasta.

    Drop the linguine into the boiling water and cook until al dente. Before draining, remember to save a small cup of the starchy pasta water: it’s the secret to achieving that creamy texture.

  4. Bring it all together.

    Add the drained pasta to the pan with the guanciale and toss well. Remove from the heat, then pour in the egg mixture, stirring quickly so the heat of the pasta gently cooks the eggs without scrambling them. Add a splash of pasta water if needed to reach a silky consistency.

  5. Cook the guanciale.

    In a large pan, cook the guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy and golden, releasing its flavorful fat. Turn off the heat and let it rest while the pasta cooks.

  6. Serve and enjoy.

    Plate immediately, top with extra Pecorino and black pepper, and enjoy this timeless Roman classic — comforting, rich, and made with just a few humble ingredients.
