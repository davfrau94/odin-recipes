Bolognese lasagna

Home

Few dishes embody the spirit of Italian comfort food quite like Lasagna alla Bolognese. Layer after layer of silky pasta, rich ragù, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese come together in perfect harmony. It’s a labor of love — one that fills the kitchen with warmth and the promise of something truly special.

Ingredients (for 4-6 servings):

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 100g (3.5 oz) ground pork (optional but traditional)
  • 100ml (⅓ cup) dry red wine
  • 400g (14 oz) canned peeled tomatoes, crushed
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)

For the béchamel:

  • 50g (3.5 tbsp) butter
  • 50g (⅓ cup) all-purpose flour
  • 500ml (2 cups) whole milk
  • A pinch of salt
  • A touch of nutmeg

For assembly:

  • 250g (9 oz) fresh lasagna sheets
  • 80g (¾ cup) grated Parmigiano Reggiano

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ragù.

    In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery, and sauté until soft and fragrant. Add the ground meats and cook until browned, breaking them apart with a spoon. Pour in the red wine and let it evaporate completely. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg, then let it simmer gently for at least 45 minutes — the longer, the better.

  2. Make the béchamel sauce.

    In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Stir in the flour and cook for a minute until smooth. Gradually add the warm milk while whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Continue stirring until the sauce thickens, then season with salt and a touch of nutmeg.

  3. Assemble the lasagna.

    Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of béchamel, followed by a layer of pasta sheets. Add a layer of ragù, a spoonful of béchamel, and a sprinkle of Parmigiano. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with béchamel and plenty of Parmigiano on top.

  4. Bake.

    Cover loosely with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for another 10–15 minutes, until golden and bubbling.

  5. Rest and serve.

    Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing — it helps the layers settle and makes each serving perfect. Serve warm, and enjoy the deep, comforting flavors of this Italian classic.
