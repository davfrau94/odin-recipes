Saffron risotto

Home

There’s an elegance in simplicity — and the "Risotto allo Zafferano" captures that perfectly. Creamy, golden, and delicately perfumed, this dish is a celebration of balance: few ingredients, yet deeply satisfying. Originating from Milan, it’s a symbol of northern Italian cuisine and the art of transforming the humble rice grain into something extraordinary.

Ingredients (serves 2-3)

  • 200g (1 cup) Arborio or Carnaroli rice
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 40g (3 tbsp) butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 100ml (⅓ cup) dry white wine
  • About 700ml (3 cups) hot vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 packet (about 0.15g) saffron threads or powder
  • 40g (⅓ cup) grated Parmigiano Reggiano
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the saffron

    Warm a small cup of broth and dissolve the saffron in it, letting it infuse while you start the risotto. This helps release its color and aroma.

  2. Sauté the onion.

    In a wide pan, heat the olive oil and half the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook slowly until soft and translucent, without letting it brown.

  3. Toast the rice.

    Add the rice to the pan and stir for about 2 minutes, coating each grain in the fat until it becomes slightly translucent at the edges. This step locks in the starch — the secret to a creamy risotto.

  4. Deglaze with wine.

    Pour in the white wine and let it evaporate completely, stirring gently.

  5. Cook the risotto.

    Begin adding the hot broth, one ladle at a time, stirring often. Wait until the liquid is almost absorbed before adding the next ladle. Continue for about 15–18 minutes, until the rice is creamy and al dente.

  6. Add the saffron.

    Halfway through cooking, pour in the saffron-infused broth and stir well. Watch as the rice turns beautifully golden and aromatic.

  7. Mantecare (the final touch).

    Remove the pan from the heat. Add the remaining butter and Parmigiano Reggiano. Stir vigorously to create a rich, velvety texture. Adjust salt if needed.

  8. Serve.

    Let the risotto rest for a minute, then plate it gently. Serve warm, spreading it slightly on the plate as tradition suggests — the perfect balance of creaminess and elegance.
